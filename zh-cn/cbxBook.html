The AA Menu

In the AA menu, accessible from the top toolbar in the book, you’ll find settings to set up layout, pagination, colors, and more. Each section of the AA menu is explained below:

AA menu → Layout and Display Settings

(top)

Layout. This is a global setting that controls how you navigate pages. You can choose:

  • Pinch to zoom: pinch the page to zoom in and out. You can drag your finger to pan. Tapping on the left and right of the page while you’re zoomed in does nothing.
  • Tap when zoomed: tap on the left and right screen edges while you’re zoomed into a page to pan it in a zig-zag like motion.

Image FX lets you tune the sharpening and colors of your comic to make it look better. This is a per-comic setting. You can tune:

  • Sharpening: this may help comics made from lower resolution scans look sharper and improve readability.
  • Contrast: this is useful to add punch to faded comic scans.
  • Exposure: brightens pages without loss in black detail.

AA menu → Page Settings

(top)

  • Orientation: allow device rotation, or lock to portrait or landscape orientations1. In-app orientation locks are not supported on devices that feature Split View such as newer iPads.

1 This is a global setting, and applies to all books.

AA menu → Page Colors

(top)

Set color of the matte around pages. Marvin provides five preset slots which you can fully customize and rename. This is a global setting, and applies to all books. To change the name or colors of a preset, tap on the eyedropper icon. Each preset color can be set to ‘auto’ for Marvin to intelligently select a matte color that matches the current page.

To change extra dimming and warmth setting for the preset, tap the warmth/fire button:

  • Extra dimming: makes the screen dimmer than the minimum physical device brightness allows. You might find this useful when reading at night.
  • Screen warmth: reduces harsh blue colors, useful when reading at night.

The Main Menu

(top)

In the main menu, accessible from the top toolbar in the book, you’ll find several tools, access to settings, book info, and your journal.

The Tools Section

(top)

  • Add a bookmark: bookmarks your location in the book. If you have multi-color bookmarks set up in EPUB settings, Marvin will ask you for the color of the bookmark to use.
  • Set a timer: set up a reading timer to remind you after you’ve been reading for a preset time.
  • Share current page: lets you share an image of the current page.

The Settings Section

(top)

  • Advanced CBx settings: access CBx-related settings not available in the AA menu. These settings are always global and apply to all books.
  • Marvin’s color scheme: lets you change Marvin’s UI colors - do not confuse this with the colors in the AA menu.

Journal and Annotations Section

(top)

Here you can access your reading journal and annotations. You can also create a new journal entry.

Lookup Section

(top)

Loop up information about the book and author on the internet.

The Thumbnails Screen

(top)

Tapping the thumbails button in the top toolbar allows you to browse your book by thumbnail. Long pressing a thumbnail zooms into the page. Any pages that are bookmarked have a round mark. Tapping on the mark allows you to modify the bookmark.

iCloud book location sync

(top)

Marvin automatically saves your location and makes it available to all other instances of Marvin on other devices. Other than being logged in to your iCloud account on your device, there’s no configuration needed. To do this, Marvin uses a tiny amount of bandwidth and space in your iCloud storage.

To navigate to a location where you left off on another device, tap the ‘SYNC’ button in the bottom toolbar of your book.

Undo and redo book location changes

(top)

When you navigate to different locations in your book (e.g. from a bookmark or thumbnails), Marvin will remember your location before the jump to let you undo back to it. The undo and redo buttons are visible as arrows in the bottom toolbar.

The scrubber

(top)

The bottom scrubber/slider allows you to jump to different pages in your comic quickly.
