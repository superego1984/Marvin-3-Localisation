The AA Menu

In the AA menu, accessible from the top toolbar in the book, you’ll find settings to adjust the layout of your books, set up pagination, fonts, colors, auto-scroll and more. Each section of the AA menu is explained below:

AA menu → Layout

(top)

  • Text size: increase or decrease the text size in the book1/5.
  • Margin size: increase or decrease the horizontal margin in the book1/2/5. Marvin remembers different margin settings for portrait and landscape orientations.
  • Line spacing: increase or decrease the line spacing in the book1/5/6.
  • Paragraph spacing: increase or decrease the spacing between paragraphs in the book1/5/6.
  • Indentation: increase or decrease paragraph indentation in the book1/5/6.
  • Uniform text size: make all the text in a book have the same size. This is NOT recommended unless you’re having trouble with very small text in the book3.
  • Switch to publisher’s/Marvin’s layout: toggle between publisher and Marvin layouts. In publisher’s layout many formatting settings such as spacing, fonts, and colors are not available. In general, it is best to stick to Marvin’s layout3.
  • Justify text: Toggle between left and full text justification4/6.
  • Hyphenate text: Enable or disable hyphenation. If the book contains language information, Marvin will try and justify properly for that language4/6.

1 This is a per book setting, and newly opened books will inherit this setting from the last opened book.

2 Note on vertical margins: In vertical scrolling mode, there are no vertical margins. In pagination mode, the vertical margin is controlled from the advanced EPUB settings panel.

3 This is a per book setting, but newly opened books will NOT inherit this setting from the last opened book.

4 This is a global setting, and applies to all books.

5 You can slide this setting label to the left to reset its value to default.

6 This setting cannot be changed when you’re in publisher’s layout mode.

AA menu → Page Settings

(top)

  • Orientation: allow device rotation, or lock to portrait or landscape orientations1. In-app orientation locks are not supported on devices that feature Split View such as newer iPads.
  • Page layout1:
    • Paginate, one column: side scrolling, page-by-page. Text is displayed in one column in all orientations.
    • Paginate, two column: side scrolling, page-by-page. Text is displayed in two columns in all orientations2.
    • Paginate, two column landscape: side scrolling, page-by-page. Text is displayed in one column in portrait mode, and two columns in landscape2.
    • Vertical scroll: text is displayed scrolling mode. This setting enables auto-scrolling.

1 This is a global setting, and applies to all books.

2 This setting may not be available on all devices. Specifically, Marvin will not display two columns if the page is physically too narrow.

AA menu → Fonts

(top)

Choose the font to use in the book. Fonts are listed in three sections: App fonts (fonts provided by Marvin), system fonts (fonts available from your device), and custom fonts. You may side-load your own custom TTF/OTF fonts from the EPUB settings panel. This is a global setting, and applies to all books. You cannot change fonts when you’re in publisher’s layout mode.

AA menu → Page Colors

(top)

Set the text, link, and background colors or background texture in the book. Marvin provides five preset slots which you can fully customize and rename. This is a global setting, and applies to all books. You cannot change colors and textures when you’re in publisher’s layout mode. To change the name or colors of a preset tap, on the eyedropper icon. To change extra dimming and warmth setting for the preset, tap the warmth/fire button:

  • Extra dimming: makes the screen dimmer than the minimum physical device brightness allows. You might find this useful when reading at night.
  • Screen warmth: reduces harsh blue colors, useful when reading at night.

AA menu → Auto Scroll

(top)

Enable auto scroll and configure it’s speed. Auto scroll is only available when you’re in vertical scrolling mode. This is a global setting, and applies to all books.

The Main Menu

(top)

In the main menu, accessible from the top toolbar in the book, you’ll find several tools, access to settings, book info, and your journal.

The Tools Section

(top)

  • Add a bookmark: bookmarks your location in the book. If you have multi-color bookmarks set up in EPUB settings, Marvin will ask you for the color of the bookmark to use.
  • Search: lets you search for text in the book. Search results can be exported to HTML files or PDFs.
  • Text-to-speech: Marvin will read your book aloud starting from your current location. You can control text-to-speech settings from the bottom toolbar in the book when Marvin starts reading your book.
  • Set a timer: set up a reading timer to remind you after you’ve been reading for a preset time.
  • Screenshot current page: takes a screenshot of the current (visible) page and lets you save it to your camera roll or share it.
  • Deep View: this is Marvin’s arfificial intelligence engine that reads your books for you and lets you look up characters, places, events, automatically build summaries, and much more. Deep View is currently available for books written in English.

The Settings Section

(top)

  • Advanced EPUB settings: access EPUB-related settings not available in the AA menu. These settings are always global and apply to all books.
  • Marvin’s color scheme: lets you change Marvin’s UI colors - do not confuse this with the book text and background colors in the AA menu.

Journal and Annotations Section

(top)

Here you can access your reading journal and annotations. You can also create a new journal entry.

Lookup Section

(top)

Loop up information about the book and author on the internet.

Tap Zones and Gestures

(top)

In Pagination Mode

  • Swiping left: moves to the next page.
  • Swiping right: moves to the previous page.
  • Swiping left/right with 2 fingers: skips 5 or 10 pages, or smart-snaps. This behavior can be changed from Main Menu → Advanced EPUB Settings → Pagination mode gestures.
  • Tapping on the left edge: moves to the next page or previous (default) page. This behavior can be changed from Main Menu → Advanced EPUB Settings → Pagination mode gestures.
  • Tapping on the right edge: moves to the next (default) page or previous page. This behavior can be changed from Main Menu → Advanced EPUB Settings → Pagination mode gestures.
  • Tapping in the middle of the page: shows or hides the toolbars.
  • Tapping in the middle of the page with 2 fingers: cycles forward through page color presets when Main Menu → Advanced EPUB Settings → Fast theme switching is enabled.
  • Tapping in the middle of the page with 3 fingers: cycles backward through page color presets when Main Menu → Advanced EPUB Settings → Fast theme switching is enabled.
  • Tapping on the top-right corner: creates a bookmark. If Main Menu → Advanced EPUB Settings → Multi-color bookmarks is enabled, you’ll be asked to choose the color of your bookmark.

In Vertical Scrolling Mode

  • Tapping on the left, right, or bottom edge: moves to the next page when Main Menu → Advanced EPUB Settings → Edge tapping in vertical mode is enabled.
  • Tapping on the top edge: moves to the previous page when Main Menu → Advanced EPUB Settings → Edge tapping in vertical mode is enabled.
  • Tapping in the middle of the page: shows or hides the toolbars.
  • Tapping in the middle of the page with 2 fingers: cycles forward through page color presets when Main Menu → Advanced EPUB Settings → Fast theme switching is enabled.
  • Tapping in the middle of the page with 3 fingers: cycles backward through page color presets when Main Menu → Advanced EPUB Settings → Fast theme switching is enabled.
  • Tapping on the top-right corner: creates a bookmark. If Main Menu → Advanced EPUB Settings → Multi-color bookmarks is enabled, you’ll be asked to choose the color of your bookmark.

Select text across pages

(top)

Start selecting text dragging your finger to the right edge. Once you start extending your selection beyond the right edge, Marvin will start scrolling to the next page. End your selection in the next page and lift your finger for Marvin to complete the page turn. This may require some practice the first time but you’ll quickly get the hang of it.

Creating and Deleting Highlights

(top)

  • To create a highlight, long press a word to enter selection mode. Drag the selection around the text you wish to highlight, and tap the pencil icon. The pencil icon with the plus (+) symbol, creates a highlight and opens the editor to attach a note or photo.
  • To delete a highlight, long press on any word in the highlight to bring up the selection menu. Tap the pencil or pencil with plus (+) symbol to delete the highlight.

The Contents Screen

(top)

Tapping the contents button in the top toolbar allows you to browse:

  • The book’s table of contents. By tapping the top menu button in the contents screen, you can toggle between ‘contents’ mode and ‘section’ mode. Contents mode is the table of contents defined in the EPUB file. Section mode lists sections by EPUB file. This mode is useful if the book has a missing table of contents in the EPUB.
  • Bookmarks. Shows you a list of bookmarks you created in a book. By tapping the top menu button in the contents screen, you can sort your bookmarks and export them.
  • Annotations. Shows you all the annotations (journal entries) you created in the book. Tapping the top menu button in the contents screen lets manage and export your annotations.
  • Image explorer. Shows you all the images and photos in the book. You can zoom, export, or export all images.

Text-to-Speech

(top)

Reads your book aloud. You can configure text-to-speech settings from Book → Main Menu → Advanced EPUB Settings → Text-to-speech.

iCloud book location sync

(top)

Marvin automatically saves your location and makes it available to all other instances of Marvin on other devices. Other than being logged in to your iCloud account on your device, there’s no configuration needed. To do this, Marvin uses a tiny amount of bandwidth and space in your iCloud storage.

To navigate to a location where you left off on another device, tap the ‘SYNC’ button in the bottom toolbar of your book - note that the bottom toolbar is not available while you are in text-to-speech mode.

Undo and redo book location changes

(top)

When you navigate to different locations in your book (e.g. from a bookmark, table of contents, or link), Marvin will remember your location before the jump to let you undo back to it. The undo and redo buttons are visible as arrows in the bottom toolbar.

The scrubber

(top)

The bottom scrubber/slider allows you to jump to different parts of the book quickly. There are two modes - ‘section’ mode and ‘book contents’ mode:

  • Section mode: Navigate within the current chapter or section only.
  • Book contents mode: Navigate across chapters and sections in the book.

There are two ways to toggle the scrubber mode:

  • Book → Main Menu → Advanced EPUB Settings → Scrubber Behavior.
  • Long press (without moving) the scrubber for about a second.

The drag marker in the scrubber is slightly different between scrubbing modes to help you quickly identify which mode you are in.
